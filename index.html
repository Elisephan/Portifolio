Education

I have a bachelor’s degree in accounting sciences obtained from National University of Rwanda in 2008 with distinction. I got auditing training from ERNST &Young Rwanda (one of the big four accounting firms worldwide) where I worked as an auditor for almost two years (2007-December 2008).

Experience

I worked as District Finance and Administration Manager with Partners in Health/Rwanda for 5 years (2011-2015). I was responsible for providing overall management of the finance and administration function at Partners In Health in Burera district/Rwanda, advising District project Director in matters regarding transport, human resource and stock management at the site. I was a focal person for Chief Human Resources Officer at the district for all employees’ related issues and benefits.

Knowledge and Skills

Fluent in spoken English and Good grasp of spoken and written French
I spent six months, February 2003-July2003 at National University of Rwanda studying English before I join my bachelor’s degree courses. This is was a university requirement. Since I started working in 2007, English has been my professional working language. I attend both face-to-face and online meetings in English with people of different English background. I write my financial reports in English and respond to donors comments satisfactorily. Therefore, I have a full professional proficiency in English. My language of study in secondary school was French, at university, we used both English and French for our lectures, and therefore I have professional working proficiency in French. I worked with a couple of sub-grantees who use French languages in their reports.


Personal Qualities

Personal and professional integrity
I am a dependable person. I work under minimum supervision and I do whatever it takes to produce quality deliverables especially when the latter are the inputs to the next level of decision-making. I adhere to my organisational policies and procedures and when information is secret, I keep it confidential. I believe in quality in everything I do.
Flexibility, initiative and a “can-do” attitude
I am flexible person. In some circumstances, I focus on the results rather than on the processes. In case of emergencies, I always judge what is right to do. I have taken many initiatives at my workplace. I initiated a social solidarity fund for employees when I was Finance and Administration in Burera District. I took an initiative of writing rules and regulations for Employees savings fund and I am currently a treasurer for the fund and do the work free of charge. I am also a secretary of gender and diversity inclusion committee at my workplace.


Internal audit and Business planning

2007-2008, I was an auditor in one of the big accounting firms (Ernst & young Rwanda). I got involved in different audit assignments for different companies and organisations. The knowledge and experience I learnt helps me to carry out any financial health check for any type of the organisation. I truly understand an internal control systems checklist. With my current employer, I audited our sub-grantees and did a couple of spot checks in Partners In health’s stores. 2016, I did a consultancy to produce a business plan for Global Alliance for Improved Nutrition organisation’s sub-grantee. As grants manager, I always review the concept for business cases to support our small farmers’ projects in our program on social and economic rights, business cases to s upport community health workers cooperatives, projects proposals submitted by our sub-grantees, etc.


Ability to work in a dispersed team

I have been working with people that are located in different places, at different level for long time. As a grants manager, I work with a range of people in my organisation and outside my organisation during a proposal budget development. I collaborate with accountants, program managers, and procurement staff in order to produce an accurate report. I worked with headquarter staff and donors on different assignments like fund balances review, budget reallocation using Microsoft teams, skype for business, zoom, etc. Ability to work under pressure and to tight deadlines – to be able to prioritise clearly In my management training courses, I have learnt how to prioritise my work in order to be productive and efficient. I have the ability to work under pressure and to tight deadlines especially when I want to meet a donor deadline report, declare statutory deductions and submitting a project proposal.
